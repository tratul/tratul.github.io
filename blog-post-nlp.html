An Empirical Survey on Crowdsourcing-Based Data Management Techniques

  • Machine Learning
  • Crowdsourcing
  • Review
  • Natural Language Processing
  • Survey

Designing a crowdsourcing platform to collect data from mass user/people through user-centric applications in order to develop Bengali knowledgebase for multilingual semantic machine translation.

Crowdsourcing platforms are an efficacious approach to connect the abilities of people to relate with human estimation for distinctive tasks like sentiment analysis and image recognition etc. Accompanying the expansion of various crowdsourcing platforms like Toluna, Ushahidi, Microworkers, Google crowdsourcing platform or for other crowdsourcing platforms a large workforce and a large knowledge base needed. Automated processes cannot completely address any important analytical duties and data management tasks. These tasks, such as recognizing images and sentiment analysis can be enhanced by using human intellectual capability. Crowd-sourced management of data has therefore become an ever-greater research field and business attention. We study four distinctive platforms in this work and conduct a broad variety of crowdsourcing data management studies. Based on this research, we then identify three primary considerations for improving data management in crowdsources.

Islam, Saima Sharleen, Md Mahmudul Hasan Bhuiyan, Zahiduddin Ahmed, and Md Al-Amin. "An Empirical Survey on Crowdsourcing-Based Data Management Techniques." In Proceedings of the International Conference on Computing Advancements, pp. 1-7. 2020.

ICCA 2020: Proceedings of the International Conference on Computing Advancements(See the work)

In this work, we surveyed only four platforms considering only three parameters. We want to continue this survey and in future we want to highlight the types of the task which are commonly used in crowdsourcing platforms. And based on the task types three parameters will be considered then. Based on this study we get to understand how the data from a distinct application is handled by a platform. In the future, the crowdsourcing platform will produce crowded information. The crowded information will then be sent into the local knowledge core. Here the crowdsourcing platforms main task will be basically categorizing all the data and later after the validation it will be validate again by a human expert. The objective of that platform will be to support the local knowledge core (LKC).
